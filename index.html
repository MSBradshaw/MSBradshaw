Michael began his PhD at CU Boulder in August 2019. He is part of Ryan Layer's lab focused on Computational Genomics. Michael is interested in how computer science and improved algorithms can be used to leverage useful information from genomic data.

Early in his undergrad at Brigham Young University, Michael worked with Alonzo Cook studying diabetic peripheral neuropathy. During this time he realized his love for research but his loathe for wetlab, prompting his switch to bioinformatics research.

Subsequently Michael researched with Samuel Payne, investigating the use of machine learning and Benford's Law for detecting fraud in high-throughput biological data.

During the summer of 2018 and from January - August 2019 Michael interned with The Max Planck Institute for Chemical Ecology in the Ian T. Baldwin Lab. During this time he worked on elucidating the genetic control of microbiome recruitment of using qualitative trait mapping

Michael also participated in numerous projects with the Bioinformatics Research Group.
